EN
\n
Mer paraguayenne/Paraguayan Sea
\xa0is a text intervention into public space by Montreal visual artist Andrew Forster in collaboration with the Montreal poet Er\xedn Moure. Installed outdoors, the wide yellow band wraps around the ground floor of Concordia University\u2019s EV (Engineering and Visual Arts) Building, facing Ste-Catherine street and McKay street in downtown Montreal.\xa0This graphic and typographic artwork in a mix of three languages\u2014French, English and Guaran\xed\u2014relays Moure\u2019s poetic translation into Frenglish and Guaran\xed of the novela in Portunhol and Guaran\xed, 
Mar Paraguayo,
 by Brazilian writer Wilson Bueno.\xa0
\n
The piece questions if it is possible to turn rigorous and complex creative practices (such as art) outwards towards public space\u2014like a skin turned inside out. How does this viscera of poetic language compete with the instrumental syntax of advertising and branding on the street? The text is not bilingual or trilingual but, rather moves in and out of three different languages making one meaning. As such, it reflects not an official politics or policy of language but a lived and creative amalgam of language and meaning reflective of the creative \u2018mix\u2019 that is Montreal's urban hybridity.\xa0

FR

Intervention textuelle dans l\u2019espace public,\xa0
Mer paraguayenne/Paraguayan Sea\xa0
est le fruit d\u2019une collaboration entre l\u2019artiste visuel montr\xe9alais Andrew Forster et la\xa0po\xe8te montr\xe9alaise Er\xedn Moure.\xa0La bande\xa0jaune de grande dimension est d\xe9ploy\xe9e \xe0 l\u2019ext\xe9rieur, enveloppant les fa\xe7ades du rez-de-chauss\xe9e du\xa0pavillon EV (g\xe9nie et arts visuels) de l\u2019Universit\xe9 Concordia qui donnent sur les rues Sainte-Catherine et McKay, au centre-ville de Montr\xe9al. Cette\xa0\u0153uvre graphique et typographique relaie le texte po\xe9tique d\u2019Er\xedn Moure, une traduction en anglais qui utilise trois langues\u2014fran\xe7ais, anglais et guaran\xed\u2014du roman\xa0
Mar Paraguayo\xa0
de l\u2019auteur br\xe9silien Wilson Bueno, \xe9crit en portunhol et guaran\xed.
\n
L\u2019\u0153uvre exprime un questionnement sur la possibilit\xe9 de tourner\xa0les pratiques\xa0cr\xe9atrices complexes et rigoureuses\xa0(comme l\u2019art) vers l\u2019ext\xe9rieur et\xa0l\u2019espace public \u2013 telle une peau retourn\xe9e. Comment ce visc\xe8re de langage po\xe9tique rivalise-t-il avec la syntaxe instrumentale des\xa0publicit\xe9s et\xa0du \xab\xa0branding\xa0\xbb\xa0de la rue? Le texte n\u2019est pas bilingue ou trilingue, mais sinue entre trois langues pour rendre un sens global. Ainsi, il refl\xe8te non pas\xa0une politique linguistique officielle, mais plut\xf4t un amalgame v\xe9cu et imaginatif de langage et de signification qui \xe9voque le m\xe9lange cr\xe9atif propre\xa0\xe0 l\u2019hybridit\xe9 urbaine montr\xe9alaise.
\n
Soutenu financi\xe8rement par l\u2019Endroit indiqu\xe9 et une bourse de perfectionnement professionnel de la CUPFA.

Andrew Forster
\xa0lives in Montreal. His work includes installation, video, performance, live public events and other collaborative and cross-disciplinary projects. His current work centers on video-video installation of movement-based performance shown in visual art and dance venues, and text-installations for public space. Forster\u2019s past work includes a performance for 75 people entitled 
En masse
 with choreographer Suzanne Miller (Tangente, Montreal); 
Ossip
, and a dance performance based on the poetry of Ossip Mandelstam (coproduction OVertigo/Tangente). He has done several performance-based works for public space including 
Cin\xe9ma
 (Soci\xe9t\xe9 des arts technologiques), a multi-media piece with live performance and live-mixed audio taking place outdoors in Place de la paix for an audience seated indoors looking out through SAT\u2019s storefront windows. Moat, a related piece, was an interactive installation about surveilance using performance and live feeds from Trafalgar Square, London (supported by Canada House and Space Studios). His critical writings about performance, design, and visual art have appeared in art and academic publications. He has also participated as an artist in several design competitions for public space, including the winning proposal in a competition for a new entrance to Place des Arts, Montreal (with architects Atelier Big City) and a competition for a video projection on the new Wilder Espace Danse in Montreal (2017).

Andrew Forster
\xa0vit \xe0 Montr\xe9al. Dans sa pratique, il utilise diff\xe9rents m\xe9dias\xa0: installations performance-vid\xe9o, performance, vid\xe9o, installations textuelles et photographie. Son travail actuel prend deux directions principales : des installations textuelles pour des espaces publics ; et  installation, performance-vid\xe9o et performance pr\xe9sent\xe9es dans des lieux d\xe9di\xe9s \xe0 l\u2019art visuel ou \xe0 la danse. Il a r\xe9alis\xe9 plusieurs projets artistiques pour des espaces publics, dont 
Cin\xe9ma
 (Soci\xe9t\xe9 des arts technologiques), pi\xe8ce multim\xe9dia avec performance et diffusion audio en direct ainsi que projection vid\xe9o, se d\xe9roulant \xe0 l\u2019ext\xe9rieur, sur la place de la Paix, pour un public assis \xe0 l\u2019int\xe9rieur de la SAT.
