Installed outdoors, the wide yellow band wraps around the ground floor of Concordia University\u2019s EV (Engineering and Visual Arts) Building, facing Ste-Catherine street and McKay street in downtown Montreal.\xa0This graphic and typographic artwork in a mix of three languages\u2014French, English and Guaran\xed\u2014relays Moure\u2019s poetic translation into Frenglish and Guaran\xed of the novela in Portunhol and Guaran\xed, <i>Mar Paraguayo,</i> by Brazilian writer Wilson Bueno.\xa0</p>\n<p>The piece questions if it is possible to turn rigorous and complex creative practices (such as art) outwards towards public space\u2014like a skin turned inside out. How does this viscera of poetic language compete with the instrumental syntax of advertising and branding on the street? The text is not bilingual or trilingual but, rather moves in and out of three different languages making one meaning. As such, it reflects not an official politics or policy of language but a lived and creative amalgam of language and meaning reflective of the creative \u2018mix\u2019 that is Montreal's urban hybridity.\xa0</p>\n<p><i>Financial support from l\u2019Endroit indiqu\xe9 and CUPFA Professional Development Grant.</i></p>\n<p>NOTE: Bueno, Wilson. <i>Paraguayan Sea</i>. Trans. from Portunhol of Mar Paraguayo. New York: Nightboat, forthcoming fall 2017.</p>\n</div></div>\n<div class="section separator">\n<div class="separator ">\n<hr/>\n</div></div>\n<div class="section title">\n<div class="section-header-wrapper">\n<h2 class="section-header">Exposition<br>\n<i>Mer paraguayenne / Paraguayan Sea</i>\n<br>\n Andrew Forster &amp; Er\xedn Moure</br></br></h2>\n</div></div>\n<div class="parbase section wysiwyg">\n<div class="rte">\n<p><b>ao\xfbt \xe0 d\xe9cembre 2017</b></p>\n<p><b>FR</b></p>\n<p>Intervention textuelle dans l\u2019espace public,\xa0<i>Mer paraguayenne/Paraguayan Sea\xa0</i>est le fruit d\u2019une collaboration entre l\u2019artiste visuel montr\xe9alais Andrew Forster et la\xa0po\xe8te montr\xe9alaise Er\xedn Moure.\xa0La bande\xa0jaune de grande dimension est d\xe9ploy\xe9e \xe0 l\u2019ext\xe9rieur, enveloppant les fa\xe7ades du rez-de-chauss\xe9e du\xa0pavillon EV (g\xe9nie et arts visuels) de l\u2019Universit\xe9 Concordia qui donnent sur les rues Sainte-Catherine et McKay, au centre-ville de Montr\xe9al. Cette\xa0\u0153uvre graphique et typographique relaie le texte po\xe9tique d\u2019Er\xedn Moure, une traduction en anglais qui utilise trois langues\u2014fran\xe7ais, anglais et guaran\xed\u2014du roman\xa0<i>Mar Paraguayo\xa0</i>de l\u2019auteur br\xe9silien Wilson Bueno, \xe9crit en portunhol et guaran\xed.</p>\n<p>L\u2019\u0153uvre exprime un questionnement sur la possibilit\xe9 de tourner\xa0les pratiques\xa0cr\xe9atrices complexes et rigoureuses\xa0(comme l\u2019art) vers l\u2019ext\xe9rieur et\xa0l\u2019espace public \u2013 telle une peau retourn\xe9e. Comment ce visc\xe8re de langage po\xe9tique rivalise-t-il avec la syntaxe instrumentale des\xa0publicit\xe9s et\xa0du \xab\xa0branding\xa0\xbb\xa0de la rue? Le texte n\u2019est pas bilingue ou trilingue, mais sinue entre trois langues pour rendre un sens global. Ainsi, il refl\xe8te non pas\xa0une politique linguistique officielle, mais plut\xf4t un amalgame v\xe9cu et imaginatif de langage et de signification qui \xe9voque le m\xe9lange cr\xe9atif propre\xa0\xe0 l\u2019hybridit\xe9 urbaine montr\xe9alaise.</p>\n<p><i>Soutenu financi\xe8rement par l\u2019Endroit indiqu\xe9 et une bourse de perfectionnement professionnel de la CUPFA.</i></p>\n</div></div>\n<div class="section separator">\n<div class="separator ">\n<hr/>\n</div></div>\n<div class="section title">\n<div class="section-header-wrapper">\n<h2 class="rte">\n<p><b>EN</b></p>\n<p><b><a adhocenable="false" href="http://reluctant.ca/" target="_blank">Andrew Forster</a></b>\xa0lives in Montreal. His work includes installation, video, performance, live public events and other collaborative and cross-disciplinary projects. His current work centers on video-video installation of movement-based performance shown in visual art and dance venues, and text-installations for public space. Forster\u2019s past work includes a performance for 75 people entitled <i>En masse</i> with choreographer Suzanne Miller (Tangente, Montreal); <i>Ossip</i>, and a dance performance based on the poetry of Ossip Mandelstam (coproduction OVertigo/Tangente). Moat, a related piece, was an interactive installation about surveilance using performance and live feeds from Trafalgar Square, London (supported by Canada House and Space Studios). His critical writings about performance, design, and visual art have appeared in art and academic publications. He has also participated as an artist in several design competitions for public space, including the winning proposal in a competition for a new entrance to Place des Arts, Montreal (with architects Atelier Big City) and a competition for a video projection on the new Wilder Espace Danse in Montreal (2017). </p>\n</div></div>\n<div class="section separator">\n<div class="separator ">\n<hr/>\n</div></div>\n<div class="parbase section wysiwyg">\n<div class="rte">\n<p><b>FR</b></p>\n<p><b><a adhocenable="false" href="http://reluctant.ca/" target="_blank">Andrew Forster</a></b>\xa0vit \xe0 Montr\xe9al. Parmi ses autres projets, on retrouve : <i>En masse</i>, performance pour 75 personnes (avec la chor\xe9graphe Suzanne Miller, \xe0 Tangente, Montr\xe9al); et <i>Ossip</i>, performance dans\xe9e inspir\xe9e de la po\xe9sie d\u2019Ossip Mandelstam (co-production O Vertigo/Tangente). Par ailleurs, il participe, comme artiste, \xe0 plusieurs concours de design, notamment la r\xe9alisation du projet laur\xe9at du concours pour une nouvelle entr\xe9e de la Place des Arts \xe0 Montr\xe9al (avec l\u2019atelier d\u2019architectes Big City); programme d\u2019identit\xe9 du Quartier des spectacles, Montr\xe9al (2004) ainsi que Concours international d\u2019id\xe9es YUL-MTL : Paysages en mouvement (2011). </p>\n</div></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div></div>\n<div class="accordion-panel section">\n<div class="c-accordion toggable">\n<div class="item ">\n<div class="group panel default">\n<h3 class="title">\n<a name="legend-expand" title="Open Er\xedn Moure ">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n                    Er\xedn Moure \r\n                </a>\n</h3>\n<div class="arrow"></div>\n</div>\n<div class="content row-fluid accordion_accordion_panel_111096835" style="display: none;">\n<div class="span12">\n<div class="parsys"><div class="parbase section wysiwyg">\n<div class="rte">\n<p><b>EN</b></p>\n<p>In 2016, poet <a adhocenable="false" href="http://erinmoure.strikingly.com/" target="_blank"><b>Er\xedn Moure</b></a>\xa0published translations from French of Fran\xe7ois Turcot\u2019s\xa0<i>My Dinosaur</i>, and from Galician of Chus Pato\u2019s\xa0<i>Flesh of Leviathan\xa0</i>and Rosal\xeda de Castro\u2019s\xa0<i>New Leaves</i>.\xa0A 40-year retrospective of her own poetry,\xa0<i>Planetary Noise: Selected Poetry</i>\xa0<i>of Er\xedn Moure</i>\xa0, edited by Shannon Maguire (Wesleyan University Press), appeared in spring 2017. Her translation from Brazilian Portunhol into Montreal Frenglish of Wilson Bueno\u2019s\xa0<i>Paraguayan Sea</i>\xa0(Nightboat Books) will appear in October, 2017, and in Galicia, her translation of Ant\xf3n Lopo\u2019s biography of Ux\xedo Novoneyra,\xa0<i>Distance of the Wolf</i>\xa0(Novoneyra Foundation), will be published in June. Moure holds two honorary doctorates for her contribution to poetry, Brandon University (2008) and Universidade de Vigo in Spain (2016). As the Woodberry Poetry Room Fellow for 2016-2017 at Harvard University, she spent nine days in April listening to the poetry of Elizabeth Bishop and Muriel Rukseyer, and reading that of Angelina Weld Grimk\xe9, in preparation for creating a new poetic work on migration, asthma, and women.</p>\n</div></div>\n<div class="section separator">\n<div class="separator ">\n<hr/>\n</div></div>\n<div class="parbase section wysiwyg">\n<div class="rte">\n<p><b>FR</b></p>\n<p>En 2016, la po\xe8te\xa0<b><a adhocenable="false" href="http://erinmoure.strikingly.com/" target="_blank">Er\xedn Moure</a>\xa0</b>a publi\xe9\xa0<i>My Dinosaur</i>,\xa0<i>Leviathan</i>\xa0et\xa0<i>New Leaves</i>, traductions anglaises de recueils \xe9crits respectivement en fran\xe7ais par Fran\xe7ois Turcot et\xa0en galicien par Chus Pato et Rosal\xeda de Castro. Au printemps 2017 a paru une r\xe9trospective de sa propre po\xe9sie intitul\xe9e\xa0<i>Planetary Noise: Selected Poetry of Er\xedn Moure</i>.\xa0Couvrant une p\xe9riode de 40 ans, l\u2019ouvrage est publi\xe9 par Wesleyan University Press sous la direction de Shannon Maguire. De plus, en juin prochain, elle publiera\xa0en\xa0Galice, sous le titre\xa0<i>Distance of the Wolf\xa0</i>(Novoneyra Foundation), une traduction de la biographie d\u2019Ux\xedo Novoneyra par Ant\xf3n Lopo. Er\xedn Moure est titulaire de\xa0deux doctorats honorifiques \u2013 un de la Brandon University (2008), au Manitoba, et l\u2019autre de l\u2019Universit\xe9 de Vigo (2016), en Espagne \u2013 pour sa contribution \xe0 la po\xe9sie.\xa0Chercheuse associ\xe9e en cr\xe9ation\xa0de la Woodberry Poetry Room de l\u2019Universit\xe9 Harvard en 2016-2017, elle\xa0y\xa0a pass\xe9 neuf jours en avril \xe0 \xe9couter la po\xe9sie d\u2019Elizabeth\xa0Bishop et de Muriel Rukseyer, ainsi qu\u2019\xe0 lire celle d\u2019Angelina Weld Grimk\xe9, afin de se pr\xe9parer \xe0 la cr\xe9ation d\u2019une nouvelle \u0153uvre po\xe9tique sur la migration, l\u2019asthme,\xa0et les femmes.</p>\n</div></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div></div>\n<div class="section separator">\n<div class="separator ">\n<hr/>\n</div></div>\n<div class="image parbase section">\n<div class=" " id="cq-image-jsp-/content/shared/en/events/finearts/fofa/2017/08/03/exhibition-andrew-forster-erin-moure/jcr:content/parsys-main/image_205916955" style="width:150px;">\n<img alt="CUPFA" class="cq-dd-image" src="/cuevents/finearts/fofa/2017/08/03/exhibition-andrew-forster-erin-moure/_jcr_content/parsys-main/image_205916955.img.jpg/1504107836452.jpg" title="CUPFA"/>\n<script type="text/javascript">\n        $(document).ready(function() {\n\t\t\t$("#6ad2b8d7-dde7-4f1d-8493-721dc000b7ccparsys-mainimage_205916955").attr("href", "");\n        });\n    </script>\n</div></div>\n</div>\n<hr/>\n<div class="row-fluid">\n<div class="span3"> Where   </div>\n<div class="span9">\r\nRoom EV 1-715, FOFA Gallery,  \r\n    <a href="/maps/sgw-campus.html?building=EV">Engineering, Computer Science and Visual Arts Integrated Complex</a> (1515 St. Catherine W.), Sir George Williams Campus<br><br/></br></div>\n</div>\n<div class="row-fluid">\n<div class="span3">Cost</div>\n<div class="span9">Admission is free</div>\n</div>\n<div class="row-fluid">\n<div class="span3">Category</div>\n<div class="span9">Arts &amp;
