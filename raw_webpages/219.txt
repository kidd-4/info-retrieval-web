even" property="content:encoded"><p><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>THE LANDLORD MUST</strong>:</span><br/>\n\u2022 deliver and maintain the dwelling in a clean condition, fit for habitation;<br/>\n\u2022 ensure that the tenant has the full and peaceful enjoyment of the dwelling;<br/>\n\u2022 perform repairs; use the Rental Board to settle disputes; minimize any damages to the tenant</p>\n\n<p><strong><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;">THE LANDLORD IS ALLOWED TO:</span></span></strong><br/>\n\u2022 ask for references, full name, current/previous addresses and date of birth (for credit check);<br/>\n\u2022 require a co-signer for students without a steady income;<br/>\n\u2022 demand the 1st month\u2019s rent (or a portion of the 1st month\u2019s rent) in advance<br/>\nTIP: avoid divulging sensitive personal information and the cost of a credit check by requesting your<br/>\nown credit check; give the landlord a copy of your credit report from Equifax. Visit www.equifax.ca<br/>\nor pick up a request form at our office.</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>ESTABLISH PAYMENT HABITS</strong></span></span>: (COMMISSION D\u2019ACC\xc8S \xc0 L\u2019INFORMATION DU QU\xc9BEC)<br/>\nIf no credit is established (for students/newcomers) you need not prove payment history, but both parties must decide how tenants\u2019 ability to pay will be assessed, such as by providing:<br/>\n\u2022 letter from financial institution stating one has honoured their obligations<br/>\n\u2022 letter from previous landlord stating one has honoured their obligations<br/>\n\u2022 other documentation from organization or company which requires payment over a period of time<br/>\nthat one has honoured their obligations;<br/>\n\u2022 relevant portions of a credit check (if credit history developed).</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>THE LANDLORD CANNOT:</strong></span></span><br/>\n\u2022 demand/collect credit card numbers, bank account numbers, social insurance number, visa or<br/>\npassport, driver\u2019s permit or health insurance card (although someone may show their license or<br/>\nhealth insurance card to confirm name and address);<br/>\n\u2022 enter the apartment without giving 24 hours notice (it may be verbal), unless the dwelling is for rent<br/>\n(consent is still required) in the event of an emergency; *see us for exact conditions<br/>\n\u2022 change the locks without the tenant\u2019s agreement (same for both parties);<br/>\n\u2022 add illegal clauses to the lease, so demand an official Quebec Lease (it\u2019s your right!);<br/>\n\u2022 ask for any type of deposits (key, damage, etc.); require post-dated cheques;<br/>\n\u2022 refuse to draw a lease up in English or French upon request.<br/>\nTIP: visit us to file a complaint if illegal or unnecessary personal information is required.</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>NOTES ON\xa0RENEWING/TRANSFERRING AND UNIVERSITY RESIDENCE</strong></span></span><br/>\n\u2022 Unless the landlord consents, a lease cannot be cancelled, only transferred/assigned;<br/>\n\u2022 Leases automatically renew, so written notice of \u201cnon-renewal\u201d is required if you want to leave:<br/>\n12 month lease: 3-6 months notice; less than 12 month or indeterminate lease: 1-2 months notice;<br/>\n\u2022 Students in university residence have virtually the same rights as off-campus tenants, but need to maintain<br/>\nfull-time status. An educational institution must use the rental board to settle disputes and cannot require<br/>\ndeposits or application fees as a landlord.</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>RENT INCREASES: (WHEN SIGNING A NEW LEASE OR RENEWING A LEASE)</strong></span></span><br/>\n\u2022 if your rent is higher than the \u201cprevious rental amount\u201d (Leaes: Section G), you must file for a \u201crent fixation<br/>\nhearing\u201d within 10 days of signing the lease;<br/>\n\u2022 If the landlord has not indicated the previous rent in Section G or you discover the actual previous rental price<br/>\nwas lower than stated in Section G, you have 2 months to apply for a \u201crent fixation hearing\u201d;<br/>\n\u2022 if you choose to reside in the dwelling, the landlord can only raise the rent once per year. Please see our<br/>\n\u201cRefuse Your Rent Increase\u201d pamphlet for more information.</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>BEFORE PROBLEMS ARISE:</strong></span></span><br/>\n\u2022 have necessary repairs written on lease before signing;<br/>\n\u2022 look for problems within first 10 days of living in the new dwelling, take photographs and follow procedure if<br/>\nrepairs are not completed after verbal demands;</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>LEASE AGREEMENT TYPES:</strong></span></span><br/>\n\u2022 NEW LEASE: Lease provided by the R\xe9gie du logement;<br/>\n\u2022 SUB-LEASE: for tenants who temporarily leave dwelling;<br/>\n\u2022 LEASE ASSIGNMENT/TRANSFER: for tenants who wish to leave their dwelling permanently;<br/>\n\u2022 ROOM TO RENT/BOARDER: (no lease, not covered under the R\xe9gie du logement): owner rents out a room in<br/>\ntheir private dwelling;<br/>\n\u2022 JOINT TENANCY AGREEMENT: all tenants on the lease create a letter agreement with other joint tenants to<br/>\nestablish: portion of rent to be paid, division of bills, exclusive use of rooms, etc.<br/>\n\u2022 OCCUPANCY AGREEMENT: tenant on the lease invites other(s) to reside in the dwelling without having them<br/>\nsign the lease with the landlord. If not more than 2 rooms are rented out by the tenant on the lease, the<br/>\noccupants are not covered by provincial housing regulations. See us for more details!</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>OTHER\xa0USEFUL HOUSING TIPS:</strong></span></span></p>\n\n<p>HEATING INCLUDED? If heating is not included in the rent, get the address of the dwelling, and contact the following numbers to determine the approximate cost.<br/>\nHydro Quebec: (514) 363-7443<br/>\nGaz Metro: (514) 598-3222 x 3</p>\n\n<p>HOUSING INSURANCE<br/>\nMeloche Monnex: 1-800-361-3821 (Concordians receive 20% off)</p>\n\n<p>GARBAGE &amp; RECYCLING ville.montreal.qc.ca/collections</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>CONTACTS</strong></span></span><br/>\nR\xe9gie du Logement: www.rdl.gouv.qc.ca / (514) 873-2245<br/>\nArnold Bennett (Housing Hotline): (514) 990-0190<br/>\nTed Wright (c/o Westmount YMCA): (514) 931-8046</p>\n\n<p><span style="color:#0000CD;"><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>QUEBEC APARTMENT SIZES</strong></span></span></p>\n\n<p>0\xbd: Room to Rent - Boarder: room in a private home with kitchen access and sometimes meals.<br/>\n1\xbd: One large room with kitchenette and bathroom.<br/>\n2\xbd: Like 1\xbd but may be double and/or L-shaped.<br/>\n3\xbd: One bedroom, living room, kitchen and bathroom. Some have a double room split in two.<br/>\n4\xbd: Two bedrooms (or one double room), living room, kitchen, and bathroom.<br/>\n5\xbd &amp; UP: Increase the number of bedrooms by one for each
