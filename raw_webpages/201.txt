Deficit, Expects Surplus in 2015-2016</h2>\n\t\n\t<p class="byline"><span class="section-badge">News</span> by <a href="/author/michelle-pucci">Michelle Pucci</a> \u2014 Published January 20, 2016 | Updated January 20, 2016 | <em class="ss-icon ss-chat"></em><a class="comment-count" data-disqus-identifier="thelink_entry_8023" href="#disqus_thread">Comments</a></p>\n\t\n\t<p><a class="twitter-follow-button" data-dnt="true" data-show-count="false" data-show-screen-name="true" href="https://twitter.com/michellempucci">Follow @michellempucci</a></p>\n\t\n\t<div class="entry-images full flexslider"><ul class="slides group">\n\t\n\t\t<li class="figure">\n\t\t\t<a class="cb" href="https://thelinknewspaper.ca/images/made/images/articles/Stock%20Images/_resized/13CA.CSU(Nikolas_Litzenberger)_900_600_90.jpg" rel="images" title="Year-end financial statements reveal a deficit src="/images/made/images/articles/Stock%20Images/_resized/13CA.CSU(Nikolas_Litzenberger)_820_547_90.jpg"/>\n\t\t\t</a>\n\t\t\t<p>Year-end financial statements reveal a deficit of $179,352, which includes costs not normally accounted for in the annual budget. File Photo Nikolas Litzenberger </p>\n\t\t</li>\n\t</ul></div>\n\t\n\t\n\t\t<div class="intro"><p>A new money-transfer system, as well as a new model for fee collection, should prevent the Concordia Student Union from running deficits like it has in the past two years.</p></div>\n\t\n\n\t\n\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\t<p>Year-end financial statements reveal a deficit of $179,352, which include costs not normally accounted for in the annual budget. According to <span class="caps">CSU</span> General Coordinator Terry Wilkings, the 2014-2015 budget expected a deficit of more than $150,000. </p>\n\n\t<p>Wilkings argued that the union actually produced a lower deficit than expected, since $85,000 of total 2015 expenses were caused by amortization\u2014which is when costs for assets are spread out over time. <span class="caps">CSU</span> computers, software, equipment, furniture purchases and costs for space improvements at Reggie\u2019s and The Hive Cafe are amortized.</p>\n\n\t<p>\u201cConsidering that the previous year went $300,000 over their budget, I think we can say we performed well,\u201d Wilkings said.</p>\n\n\t<p>The 2013-2014 audited statements revealed a deficit of more than $330,000, when a surplus of $60,000 was expected.</p>\n\n\t<p>\u201cWe projected it and consciously said this is how much we\u2019re going to be losing year-over-year,\u201d Wilkings said. \u201cBut the whole understanding is that we were going to need to create some sort of financial stability for the organization.\u201d</p>\n\n\t<p>Major expenses from the Student Space, Accessible Education and Legal Contingency fund include the cost of relocating the CSU\u2019s advocacy office, an undisclosed settlement to the Canadian Federation of Students and lawyers\u2019 fees. The <span class="caps">SSAELC</span> fund is an overhaul of the old Student Centre fund, which was created with the intention of buying a student-run building or space downtown. </p>\n\n\t<p><strong>Changing fee structures</strong></p>\n\n\t<p>During <span class="caps">CSU</span> general elections in March 2015, students voted in favour of reorganizing the union\u2019s financing structure, increasing the amount of money paid by students to the CSU\u2019s operating budget from $1.75 per credit to $1.97 per credit. Students also voted in favour of indexing <span class="caps">CSU</span> fees to the Consumer Price Index to prevent future deficits. </p>\n\n\t<p>Wilkings said the <span class="caps">CSU</span> is expecting a small surplus by the end of his term.</p>\n\n\t<p>The total amount of fees paid to the union haven\u2019t changed from the previous year, at $3.80 per credit. But the union reorganized the way it collects fees by lowering the Student Space fee and by reallocating fees specifically for its Housing and Job Bank, Advocacy Centre and Legal Information Clinic.</p>\n\n\t<p><em>2014-2015 Fees</em><br/>\nGeneral: $1.75<br/>\nNon-Academic Clubs: $0.25<br/>\n<span class="caps">SSAELC</span>: $1.50<br/>\nAdvocacy Centre: $0.30<br/>\nTotal: $3.80</p>\n\n\t<p><em>2015-2016 Fees</em><br/>\n<span class="caps">CSU</span> operations: $1.97<br/>\nAdvocacy Centre: $0.21<br/>\n<span class="caps">HOJO</span>: $0.20<br/>\nLegal Information Clinic: $0.17<br/>\n<span class="caps">CSU</span> Clubs: $0.25<br/>\n<span class="caps">SSAELC</span>: $1.00<br/>\nTotal: $3.80</p>\n\n\t<p>In the past, the <span class="caps">CSU</span> collected fees that weren\u2019t restricted to offices like <span class="caps">HOJO</span>, Legal Information Clinic and the Advocacy Centre. Funds that weren\u2019t used would fall back into the general operating budget.</p>\n\n\t<p>\u201cIt was not as transparent,\u201d Wilkings said, referring to a controversy in 2013 when <span class="caps">CSU</span> executives were asked to apologize for throwing a party not stipulated in the budget at the end of their term, which cost $9,000. Wilkings said the move to more restricted funding began shortly after.</p>\n\n\t<p><strong>Real-time fee collection</strong></p>\n\n\t<p>The new Student Information System has also changed the way the union receives its fees.</p>\n\n\t<p>Since the new <span class="caps">SIS</span> software was adopted, the <span class="caps">CSU</span> and other fee-collecting groups are given their fees as soon as students pay tuition, Wilkings said. </p>\n\n\t<p>\u201cIt\u2019s addressed our cash flow issue, because usually we would have to wait until after October to collect our first round of fees,\u201d he added.</p>\n\n\t<p>In the past, the <span class="caps">CSU</span> wouldn\u2019t receive the money collected by Concordia from student tuition until after the course add/drop deadline. This meant that every semester the <span class="caps">CSU</span> needed to ensure that they had enough cash in their accounts from the previous semester to pay for the first month\u2019s worth of salaries and other expenses, including orientation.</p>\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\n\t\n\t\n\t\n\t\n\t<!-- AddThis Button BEGIN -->\n<div class="addthis_toolbox
