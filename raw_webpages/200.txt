href="https://thelinknewspaper.ca/images/made/images/articles/Volume_36/News/_resized/23NEWS.asfa(jonathan_cook)_900_597_90.jpg" rel="images" title="On Wednesday night, the CSU council passed a motion ">\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t<img alt="" src="/images/made/images/articles/Volume_36/News/_resized/23NEWS.asfa(jonathan_cook)_820_544_90.jpg"/>\n\t\t\t</a>\n\t\t\t<p>On Wednesday night, the CSU council passed a motion demanding that Concordia change a bylaw preventing students with formal disciplinary charges from serving on the Board of Governors and Senate, as well as other committees.  Photo Noelle Didierjean</p>\n\t\t</li>\n\t</ul></div>\n\t\n\t\n\t\t<div class="intro"><p>The rights of students have been a topic of debate over the past year at Concordia University.</p></div>\n\t\n\n\t\n\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\t<p>First, the question was whether students can strike.</p>\n\n\t<p>Now, the Concordia Student Union is challenging a university bylaw that could prevent students reprimanded for striking from sitting on the Board of Governors and Senate\u2014two of Concordia\u2019s most important decision-making bodies. </p>\n\n\t<p>On Wednesday night, the <span class="caps">CSU</span> council passed a motion demanding that Concordia change the bylaw in question, which would also prevent students with formal disciplinary charges from serving on other committees.</p>\n\n\t\n\t\t<div class="figure alignright">\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t<a class="cb" href="https://thelinknewspaper.ca/images/made/images/articles/Volume_36/News/_resized/23ca.studentprotesters_267_589_90.jpeg" rel="images" title=""><img alt="" src="/images/made/images/articles/Volume_36/News/_resized/23ca.studentprotesters_267_589_90.jpeg"/></a>\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\t\t</div>\n\t\t <p></p>\n\n\t<p>The motion stated legal action will be taken if the bylaw is not altered in accordance with Quebec\u2019s act on accredited student associations. Article 32 of this law says that student associations like the <span class="caps">CSU</span> \u201calone\u201d have the power to decide who sits on these university bodies.  </p>\n\n\t<p>\u201c[The law] makes it very explicit the autonomy lies with the student association to make the judgment call,\u201d said Terry Wilkings, general coordinator of the <span class="caps">CSU</span>. </p>\n\n\t<p>The university bylaw is being scrutinized because as many as 22 student-protesters have recently received letters of reprimand\u2014the lowest form of consequence\u2014from independent tribunal panels for disrupting classes in last spring\u2019s strikes. </p>\n\n\t<p>Given that these students are also undergraduate leaders, the letters of reprimand will prevent them from representing students\u2014despite being appointed to do just that.</p>\n\n\t<p>Wilkings met with university president Alan Shepard on Thursday afternoon to discuss the matter. </p>\n\n\t<p>\u201cI think it was a productive conversation,\u201d Wilkings said. \u201cThere wasn\u2019t any commitments made.\u201d </p>\n\n\t<p>Shepard, in an interview on Wednesday, said he informed the <span class="caps">CSU</span> he would review the bylaw. He had no comment on any pending litigation. Wilkings is also planning to meet with Frederica Jacobs, secretary general of Concordia, to find possible informal resolution. </p>\n\n\t<p>There is no conflict between the university bylaw and the Quebec law, according to university spokesperson Chris Mota. \u201cThe <span class="caps">CSU</span> has every right to nominate whoever they like,\u201d she said. \u201cBut we as an institution have every right to set criteria for eligibility.\u201d </p>\n\n\t<p>She explained that the language in the bylaws will be reviewed to make it clearer, but any student who saw a charge upheld by a tribunal will not be eligible to sit on the various university bodies next year. </p>\n\n\t<p>The <span class="caps">CSU</span> has been researching eligibility rules for other universities throughout Quebec, Wilkings said. According to him, they have found that Concordia is the only institution to have a bylaw preventing students with a disciplinary charge from serving on various bodies. </p>\n\n\t<p>\u201cThese barriers exist [here],\u201d he said. \u201cThey don\u2019t exist throughout the province.\u201d</p>\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\n\t\n\t\n\t\n\t\n\t<!-- AddThis
